Squarespace vs. Custom Website: The Real Cost Breakdown for 2026

April 24, 2026 · 7 min read

On the surface, Squarespace looks cheap. Twenty-three bucks a month for a "professional" website -- what's not to love? But if you've actually sat down to build one, you know the real cost shows up somewhere else: your weekend, your content, your patience, and eventually, an annual bill that quietly doubles.

A custom website sounds expensive by comparison, but the math isn't always what you think. Below is the honest side-by-side: what each option costs in year one, year three, and year five -- plus the hidden costs that nobody mentions until it's too late.

The 30-Second Answer

Squarespace costs about $276 to $660 per year, forever. A custom Sitebolt website costs $1,500 once, plus $100-$200/year in hosting and domain. Break-even happens in year 3, and the custom site doesn't look like every other Squarespace template on the internet.

Quick math: Three years of Squarespace ($828-$1,980) ≈ the one-time cost of a Sitebolt custom build ($1,500). After year 3, the custom site is pure savings -- and you actually own it.

Squarespace: What You're Actually Paying

Squarespace advertises plans starting at $16/month (Personal), but that plan is useless for most businesses. It limits templates, blocks e-commerce, and won't let you run a real small business site. The plans most people actually buy:

  • Business plan: $23/month ($276/year) -- needed for custom CSS, forms, and basic e-commerce
  • Commerce Basic: $28/month ($336/year) -- if you're selling products
  • Commerce Advanced: $55/month ($660/year) -- abandoned cart recovery, subscriptions

On top of the subscription, you'll also pay for:

  • Domain: $20-$35/year (Squarespace's domain pricing is higher than GoDaddy or Namecheap)
  • Premium fonts or Unsplash+ images: $0-$100/year if you want anything beyond the free library
  • Third-party apps: Booking, advanced forms, chat widgets -- $5-$30/month each
  • Transaction fees: 3% on the Business plan if you sell anything

Realistic first-year Squarespace cost: $400 - $900.

Custom Website: What You're Actually Paying

"Custom website" covers a huge range. A bespoke agency build can run $20K-$50K. A solo freelancer might charge $3K-$8K. A flat-fee service like Sitebolt comes in at $1,500. The actual costs:

  • Design and build: $1,500 (Sitebolt flat fee) up to $25K+ (agency)
  • Hosting: $5-$20/month on Cloudflare, Netlify, or similar = $60-$240/year
  • Domain: $10-$20/year
  • Email: $6-$12/user/month if you want hello@yoursite.com
  • Updates later: Depends on how the site was built; static sites are nearly free to host and easy to edit

Realistic first-year custom cost (Sitebolt tier): $1,600 - $1,800.

The Hidden Cost: Your Time

The number most Squarespace-vs-custom comparisons ignore is the time you spend. Even with a template, setting up a real business site on Squarespace is a 40-80 hour project. You'll evaluate templates, swap colors and fonts, wrangle the image library, rewrite every headline twice, figure out why the mobile view is broken, and argue with the block editor at least a dozen times.

If your time is worth $50/hour, that "cheap" $23/month site just cost you $2,000-$4,000 in opportunity cost in year one alone. If your time is worth more -- $100+/hour as a consultant, lawyer, doctor, contractor -- the DIY math gets ugly fast.

When Squarespace Actually Makes Sense

We won't pretend Squarespace is useless. It's a fine choice if you match all of these:

  • You have $0 budget and time you don't mind burning
  • You're a hobbyist, side-project, or pre-revenue
  • You're okay looking like every other Squarespace site in your niche
  • You plan to heavily edit content yourself, often

For photographers, wedding planners, or personal portfolios, Squarespace is perfectly reasonable. For small businesses trying to compete for local search traffic, it's an expensive treadmill.

When Custom Makes Sense

A custom build beats Squarespace when:

  • You want to not look like everyone else in your industry
  • You value your time at more than $40/hour
  • You're keeping this site for 3+ years (most small businesses do)
  • You want page speed and SEO performance Squarespace can't match
  • You don't want to learn a block editor to publish a simple update

Custom is also the only option if you want truly portable content. A Squarespace site is trapped in Squarespace -- you can't move it to another host if their prices jump or their service declines. A custom HTML site lives anywhere: Cloudflare, Netlify, AWS, your own server.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Squarespace cheaper than hiring someone to build a custom website?

In year one, Squarespace is cheaper on paper ($276-$900 vs $1,500 for a Sitebolt custom build). But by year three, the total cost of Squarespace matches or exceeds the custom build -- and you still don't own the site. Over 5 years, custom is 40-60% cheaper than Squarespace Business, plus you get unique design and better SEO performance.

How much does Squarespace really cost per year in 2026?

The advertised $16/month Personal plan is not realistic for a business site. Most businesses need the Business plan at $23/month ($276/year) or Commerce Basic at $28/month ($336/year). Add domain, apps, and transaction fees and the real annual cost is $400-$900.

Can I move my Squarespace site to a custom website later?

Not easily. Squarespace content is locked into their platform. You can export a limited amount of content (blog posts, basic pages) but the design, layout, and most functionality don't transfer. You'll essentially start over when you move off Squarespace. A custom HTML site is portable to any host.

Will a custom website rank better in Google than Squarespace?

Generally yes, for two reasons. First, custom sites load faster (Core Web Vitals affect rankings) because they're not carrying Squarespace's overhead. Second, custom sites give you full control over meta tags, schema markup, and page structure -- all factors in SEO. Squarespace sites can rank, but you're fighting a slower, heavier platform.

Is there a Squarespace alternative that's actually cheaper long-term?

Yes. A custom-built static site hosted on Cloudflare or Netlify costs $100-$200/year to run (domain + hosting), compared to $276-$900/year for Squarespace Business. The upfront cost is higher ($1,500 for a Sitebolt custom build), but total cost crosses over in year 2-3.
