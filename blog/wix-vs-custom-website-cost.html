Wix vs Custom Website: The Real 5-Year Cost

April 28, 2026 · 9 min read

Wix advertises websites starting at $17/month. A custom website starts at $1,500 once. On the surface those numbers feel non-comparable -- one is rent, the other is ownership. But when you actually do the math over a real business lifecycle (5 years), Wix is almost always more expensive, slower, and harder to migrate away from.

This isn't a hatchet job on Wix. It's a great tool for the people it's built for: hobbyists, side projects, and people who genuinely want to drag-and-drop their own pages on a Saturday afternoon. But for a serious small business, the trade-offs aren't what the marketing makes them look like. Here's the full breakdown.

The 5-Year Cost: Wix vs Custom

Let's compare a typical small-business setup. Wix's "Business" plan ($36/month) is the realistic starting point for anyone selling services or products -- the cheaper plans don't allow ecommerce or remove Wix branding properly.

Where Wix Actually Wins

Wix is a legitimately good choice if any of these are true:

  • You'll edit the site weekly. Wix's editor is genuinely usable. If you want to swap photos, write blog posts, or tweak copy regularly without touching code, Wix removes that friction.
  • You need it live in a weekend, period. Even the fastest custom builds take a couple of weeks. Wix can be live tonight.
  • The site is a hobby, side project, or test. If revenue from the site is uncertain, paying $17-36/month is a smaller commitment than $1,500 upfront.
  • You don't have anyone -- not even a freelancer -- to call when something breaks. Wix gives you a single throat to choke for everything: hosting, software, support.

For roughly that profile, Wix is the right tool. The trouble starts when a serious business uses Wix because they think it's the cheaper option and discover later that it isn't.

Where Custom Wins (and It's Most Cases)

1. Speed and SEO

Wix sites are notoriously heavy. The platform loads megabytes of editor scripts, third-party trackers, and styling that visitors never see. Lighthouse scores on Wix sites typically land in the 30-60 range on mobile. A clean custom build hits 90+.

This isn't cosmetic. Google has used page speed and Core Web Vitals as ranking signals since 2021. A slower site ranks lower. A site that ranks lower gets less traffic. Less traffic means fewer leads. The compounding cost over 5 years is enormous.

2. Design freedom

Every Wix site looks like a Wix site. The templates have a particular vocabulary -- the same hero layouts, the same testimonial blocks, the same "About" page structures. Visitors who've seen any Wix site recognize them immediately, and the resemblance reads as generic.

A custom build can look like literally anything. You're not locked to a template's grid or a theme's available components. The visual identity is yours, not Wix's.

3. Ownership and lock-in

This is the one Wix doesn't advertise. You cannot export a Wix site. If you decide to leave -- bad service, rising prices, you outgrew the platform -- you have to rebuild from scratch on whatever you migrate to. Your URLs, your SEO authority, your forms, your blog posts: rebuild all of it.

A custom site is just HTML, CSS, and a domain. Hosting can move from Cloudflare to Netlify to your own server in an afternoon. Nobody owns the site but you.

4. No platform decisions for you

When Wix decides to change a feature, raise a price, deprecate an app, or push a new editor, you live with it. In late 2024 they pushed every legacy customer onto a new editor that broke a lot of older designs. Custom sites don't have a platform overlord.

The Honest Answer for Most Small Businesses

If you're running a real business -- a contractor, a clinic, a law firm, a restaurant, a B2B services company -- a custom site is almost always the better long-term choice. The math works, the SEO works, and you own what you're paying for. Wix's value is in zero-commitment speed, not in being cheap.

The break-even point. Wix Business is cheaper than a $1,500 custom site for the first 3.5 years. After that, custom is cheaper forever. If you expect the business to outlive 3-4 years, custom wins on cost alone -- before you even count the SEO and design wins.

What About Wix vs Other Builders?

Most of the math holds for Squarespace, Webflow, and Shopify too. The pricing is in the same range; the lock-in is similar; the speed is comparable. We've written a longer breakdown of Squarespace vs custom websites if you're weighing those two specifically.

Webflow is the closest thing to a "custom" site you can get on a builder -- the markup is reasonably clean and the design freedom is real. But you still pay the monthly subscription forever, and you're still locked to Webflow's hosting.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Wix or a custom website cheaper?

Wix Business is cheaper for roughly the first 3.5 years ($36/month adds up to $1,512 by month 42). After that a custom site is cheaper forever, since the build cost is one-time and hosting is essentially free. Over 5 years a typical Wix Business site costs $2,220+ vs roughly $1,560 for a custom build.

Can I move my site off Wix later?

Not really. Wix doesn't allow you to export the site code or move it to another host. Leaving Wix means rebuilding from scratch on whatever platform you choose next. This is the biggest hidden cost -- a few years of investment in your Wix site doesn't transfer.

Why do Wix sites rank lower on Google?

Wix sites tend to load slowly because the platform injects large amounts of editor and tracking code that visitors don't need. Page speed and Core Web Vitals are confirmed Google ranking signals. Custom-coded sites typically score 90+ on Lighthouse mobile vs 30-60 for Wix.

Is a custom website worth it for a small business?

For a business that plans to operate for more than three years and depends on its website for leads or sales, yes. The math favors custom on cost alone after year four, and the SEO, speed, and design ownership wins start day one. For a hobby, a side project, or a short-term test, Wix is fine.

How much does a custom website cost?

Pricing ranges widely. Freelance custom builds run $2,500-$7,500. Boutique agencies run $5,000-$25,000. Larger agencies run $25,000-$100,000+. Flat-fee services like Sitebolt deliver custom websites for $1,500 in 14 days, including design, build, and launch.

How long does a custom website take to build vs Wix?

Wix can be live the same day if you have all your content ready. A custom build takes longer because actual design and code are involved. Freelancers typically take 6-12 weeks; agencies take 2-4 months; flat-fee services like Sitebolt deliver in 14 days from intake.
